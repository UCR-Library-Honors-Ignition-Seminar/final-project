# Presentation & Poster Guide

**Week 10 Final Presentations**

---

## Option A: Presentation (5-7 minutes)

A live presentation during Week 10.

### What to include
1. **What you made** — show it live if possible (run the sketch, hold up the object, play the game)
2. **The skills you combined** — briefly explain each and why you chose to combine them
3. **One moment from your process** — a screenshot, photo, or story about something difficult or surprising
4. **What it means to you** — end with one sentence about why you made this

### Tips
- Practice your demo before class — live demos can fail. Have a backup (video recording or screenshots)
- 5-7 minutes is short. Pick the most interesting thread, not everything
- It's okay to say "I'm not sure" or "this didn't work the way I planned"
- Q&A follows each presentation

### For digital projects
- Test on the classroom computer/display before your slot
- If your project uses a webcam or microphone, arrive early to set up
- Have a backup video of your project

### For physical projects
- Bring the physical object
- Have photos/video showing it in action

## Option B: Poster

A printed or digital poster displayed during Week 10's open session.

### Poster content

| Section | What to write |
|---------|--------------|
| **Title** | Your project's name |
| **The Skills** | Which course skills you combined and how |
| **Process** | 2-3 images showing your making process |
| **The Work** | Screenshot, photo, or QR code linking to the project |
| **What I Learned** | 2-3 sentences about the most meaningful discovery |

### Poster specs (if printing)
- Recommended size: 24" x 36"
- At least 24pt font for body text
- Export as PDF for printing

### Where to print
- [UCR Library poster printing services](https://guides.lib.ucr.edu/poster)


### Digital poster
A digital poster (PDF on a laptop) is also accepted. Canva has free poster templates at [canva.com](https://www.canva.com).

## Grading: Presentation/Poster (10% of final grade)

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Clarity** | Someone unfamiliar with the project understands what it is |
| **Shows the work** | The project itself is visible, runnable, or physically present |
| **Process is visible** | Evidence of how it was made, not just the final result |
| **Engages the audience** | Q&A shows you understand your own project |
